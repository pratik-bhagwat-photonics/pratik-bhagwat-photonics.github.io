My research focuses on experimental photophysics, where optical spectroscopy is used to investigate the physical processes that govern the behaviour of materials after photoexcitation. My work combines ultrafast spectroscopy, thin-film fabrication, optical characterization, and data analysis to study excited-state dynamics in low-dimensional materials.

---

## Current Research

### Time-resolved Exciton and Spin Dynamics in Chiral Two-Dimensional Lead-Halide Perovskites

My master's research at the Division of Chemical Physics, Lund University, investigated exciton and spin dynamics in chiral two-dimensional lead-halide perovskites using femtosecond transient absorption spectroscopy. The work involved the synthesis and fabrication of chiral and achiral thin films, polarization-resolved pump–probe measurements, and transient absorption data analysis to understand ultrafast photophysical processes.

**Techniques**

- Femtosecond transient absorption spectroscopy
- Polarization-resolved pump–probe measurements
- Thin-film synthesis and fabrication
- Python, MATLAB, and Surface Xplorer for data analysis

## Previous Research

### Hybrid Polymer Nanocomposites for EMI Shielding

During my MSc in Physics, I worked on PVDF-based polymer nanocomposites containing porous carbon and Fe₃O₄ nanoparticles for electromagnetic interference shielding. The project involved materials synthesis, structural characterization, and microwave measurements to investigate shielding performance.

### ZnO Thin Films

My undergraduate research focused on the synthesis and characterization of ZnO thin films. This work introduced me to experimental materials research and optical characterization, forming the foundation for my later transition into photonics.
